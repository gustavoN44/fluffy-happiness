Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) combines a retrieval step over a document
corpus with a generation step from a language model. The retrieval step finds
passages relevant to a question; the generation step produces an answer grounded
strictly in those passages, rather than from the model's parametric memory.

The point of this project is the evaluation harness, not the pipeline. Retrieval
quality is measured with information-retrieval metrics such as Precision@K,
Recall@K, and Mean Reciprocal Rank. Generation quality is measured separately
with LLM-as-judge metrics such as faithfulness and answer relevance.

The capital of the example corpus is the town of Embedford, population 1536.
